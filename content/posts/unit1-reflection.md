\## Introduction

Information Science sits at the fascinating intersection of people, technology, and data. As I begin this course, I see it as the study of how information is created, organized, managed, and used—a discipline that shapes everything from library databases to the algorithms that power our daily digital interactions. This journal will track my journey into understanding these complex systems.



<!--more-->



\## My Reflection

Learning about the scope of Information Science was eye‑opening. I never realized that concepts like metadata, classification, and user behavior were central to the digital systems I use every day. The idea that algorithms are not neutral but reflect human decisions and biases really stuck with me.



\## Challenges I Faced

The theoretical frameworks (like "information ecology" and "information behavior") felt abstract at first. However, connecting them to real‑world examples—such as how I search for files on my computer or how Netflix recommends shows—helped make them concrete.



\## What I’m Looking Forward To

I’m excited to explore how these principles apply to emerging technologies like AI and how we can design information systems that are more ethical and inclusive.
