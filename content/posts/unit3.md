### What I Learned About Failure
This unit taught me two big things: how to fail better and the difference between shame and guilt.


First, fail better. Failure is not always bad. The Knight Capital case showed how one small mistake caused a huge loss. The real problem was the system, not just the person. We should not blame one person. We should fix the system and learn for next time. That is failing better.

 
Second, shame vs guilt. Shame makes you think “I am bad.” Guilt makes you think “what I did was bad.” Shame hides mistakes. Guilt helps you fix them. In real life, I make sure i will not feel shame. Now I try to feel guilt mostly. It helps me grow.



In my group, I was not the most working. But i was the most efficient one I managed the group very well i maintained good communication with all. But all these learning was not executed at its best presentations. Things did not go as planned. That is okay. Failure is part of learning. I cannot control everything. I can only control how I respond.

I did not blame anyone or anything for it as i did my best but it was not the best presentation. The only thing I felt bad for was our topic was really informative but everyone did not get it.



As an Information Science student, I will take these lessons forward. I will fail better, choose guilt over shame, and keep appreciating little success.
